What is DIF?

DIF is a digital identity firewall—a layer that sits between you and the internet. It doesn’t replace your existing accounts; it reinforces them. Your real identity lives on your device, in encrypted storage. Only a public fingerprint and verification signals are shared to the cloud.

Local-first by default

Your private fingerprint, identity graph and sensitive details stay on your device in IndexedDB—never shipped as plain text to any server.

Public fingerprint, private story

DIF publishes only hashed, encrypted fingerprints to the cloud so apps can verify you—without ever seeing your raw data.

Verification without friction

Link and verify your social accounts (like Instagram, X, email) once, then re-use that trust across platforms with a single DIF identity.

How DIF protects your identity

Under the hood, DIF behaves like a firewall: it filters what the outside world can see, while you maintain the full identity vault locally.

Step 1

Create your identity vault

DIF generates a private fingerprint keypair and stores it securely on your device (IndexedDB / secure storage).

  • • No server-side passwords
  • • Key material never leaves your device
  • • Works with passkeys / biometrics

Step 2

Publish a public fingerprint

DIF derives a one-way public fingerprint and pushes only that to the cloud, alongside optional encrypted metadata.

  • • Firebase stores encrypted fingerprints
  • • No raw PII is required
  • • You can revoke and regenerate anytime

Step 3

Verify once, reuse everywhere

DIF uses verification flows (like Instagram bio codes, future passkey checks, etc.) to prove accounts are really yours.

  • • Link multiple platforms to one DIF identity
  • • Share a single DIF link instead of 10 handles
  • • Apps can trust DIF without storing your login

The future of DIF

DIF is not just a tool—it’s an identity layer we want creators, developers and everyday users to build on. The roadmap focuses on passwordless auth, stronger social verification and zero-knowledge scoring.

Near term

Questions, answered.

A quick snapshot of what DIF is and isn’t.

Is DIF another social network?

No. DIF doesn’t replace your accounts. It acts like a digital passport that proves those accounts are really yours, so apps don’t have to constantly re-KYC or harvest more data.

Where is my identity actually stored?

Your core identity vault lives in your browser/device (IndexedDB or secure local storage). The cloud only sees encrypted, minimal fingerprints and verification flags.

Can I move my identity to another device?

Yes. DIF is designed to work with QR + passkey flows so you can securely re-hydrate your identity vault on a new browser without exposing your private keys to any server.
